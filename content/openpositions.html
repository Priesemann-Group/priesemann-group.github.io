Open positions

At any time, we welcome applications from creative researchers at all levels (Bachelor’s and Master’s thesis, PhD, PostDoc, team leader). Our aim is to understand learning and self-regulation (and their failure) in social and neural networks.

Open positions (general)

Join us to study learning in neural networks and the self-regulation of social networks and information spread.

Research themes

Neural learning: We particularly focus on unsupervised, local learning rules in the framework of efficient encoding, predictive coding, and homeostatic self-regulation. We use approaches from information theory, and non-linear dynamics to derive a priori the theory of how networks should ideally learn in a self-organized manner. In collaboration with experimentalists, we then cross-check the theoretical predictions, using information theory and Bayes inference. The aim is to develop a theory of local learning in living neural networks.

Social self-regulation & (mis)information: To study the self-regulation of social networks and the spread of (mis)information, we curated a large social-media data set (Telegram), which allows novel insights into the spread of information and misinformation. Complementarily, we make use of the outbreak of COVID-19, which posed a major challenge to societies and individuals – but also provided research with an extremely rich behavioral and epidemiological data set to study collective human behavior. If you want to join our team, also as PostDoc or team leader to develop your own research profile in our group with your own students, let us know. We can offer a long research perspective (3+3 years), a vibrant team, and highly motivated Bachelor, Master and PhD students.

How to apply

Please send a single PDF including: letter of motivation, CV, transcript of records, and names of referees to application.priesemannATds.mpg.de.

The motivation letter (1–2 pages) should summarize past achievements, skills, and interests, and include a short outline (~½ page) of “your research interest” (the topic you wish to pursue with us). The short outline does not need to be a final research proposal; it serves as basis for us to see the potential fit, and to discuss with you potential research projects in an interview.

Inclusivity

We encourage applicants from all backgrounds and genders to contact us.

PostDoc or PhD position (x/m/f): Synaptic Dynamics Deadline: Sept 30, 2024

How does the synaptic vesicle cycle shape plasticity and information transfer?

The vesicle cycle acts as a filter and strongly shapes information transfer at synapses. Within the Göttingen Campus (see SFB 1286), we investigate how multiple timescales in the vesicle cycle contribute to efficient pre-synaptic transduction.

Reference:

  • Jaehne et al., Cell Reports, 2021.

PhD or PostDoc (x/m/f): Hierarchical Information Processing Across Species Deadline: Sept 30, 2024

How do networks self-organize to integrate information from edges to objects and context across species?

We study cross-species organizational principles (mammals, birds) combining data analysis (with collaborators in Bochum), information theory, and theoretical work (modeling or analytical).

Relevant references:

  • Rudelt et al., arXiv, 2023.
  • Wibral, Lizier & Priesemann, Frontiers in Robotics and AI, 2015.
  • Cramer et al., Nature Communications, 2020.
