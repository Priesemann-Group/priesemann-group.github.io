Two Fellow or PostDoc positions: Complexity, Information, and Societal Dynamics Deadline: November 11, 2025

To study the self-regulation of social networks and the spread of (mis)information, we curated a large social-media data set (Telegram), which allows novel insights into the spread of information and misinformation. Complementarily, we make use of the outbreak of COVID-19, which posed a major challenge to societies and individuals – but also provided research with an extremely rich behavioral and epidemiological data set to study collective human behavior. If you want to join our team as PostDoc, or independent Fellow to develop your own research profile in our group with your own students, let us know. Postdoctoral Fellow: MEG and Cognition of Interaction Deadline: November 23, 2025

We are seeking a highly motivated and talented Postdoctoral Researcher to develop and implement a cutting-edge dyadic interaction platform allowing for OPM-MEG recordings in pairs of interacting human subjects.

Research Focus

You will have the exciting opportunity to establish neural recordings in pairs of freely interacting human subjects, pushing the boundaries of our knowledge in this field. Our unique laboratory setting, featuring two synchronized OPM-MEG devices with high channel counts and a tailor-made magnetically shielded room with ultra-low residual magnetic fields, provides an ideal environment for innovative research.

This opportunity presents a collaboration with Prof. Michael Wibral, and with our Collaborative Research Centre (CRC) 1528 “Cognition of Interaction” where you will have the chance to explore novel dyadic games unfolding in continuous time with full mutual visibility and advance our understanding of the neural basis of human interaction.

Your Profile

We are looking for a Postdoctoral Researcher with a PhD in Physics, Engineering, Neuroscience or a related field, complemented by strong communication and programming skills and a passion for collaborative research in neuroscience.

Prior experience with EEG or MEG, particularly OPM-MEG, and a background in designing and organizing studies in neuroscience are desirable. Additionally, knowledge in optics, electronics, or mechanical design would be a plus.

We encourage interested applicants from all backgrounds and genders to contact us.

PhD position: Resilience of water-limited ecosystems (CASUS–HZDR & MPI-DS) Deadline: October 27, 2025

We are seeking an enthusiastic and talented PhD candidate to join a collaborative project between CASUS–HZDR (Görlitz, Germany) and the Max Planck Institute for Dynamics and Self-Organization (Göttingen, Germany), jointly supervised by Ricardo Martinez-Garcia and Viola Priesemann.

The project will investigate the resilience of water-limited ecosystems under global change scenarios. In particular, we aim to understand the relationships between spatial self-organization and ecosystem resilience, both in simulated and remote-sensing data, and using a combination of pattern-formation theory, functional network analysis, and Bayesian inference tools. The position is based primarily at CASUS (Görlitz), with regular research stays in Göttingen. The starting date is flexible, but should be as soon as possible.

More information and application link: hzdr.de/db/Cms?pOid=75779

The Helmholtz-Zentrum Dresden–Rossendorf and the Max Planck Society are committed to increasing the participation of individuals with disabilities and therefore encourage applications from qualified candidates. Information for all applicants (Bachelor/Master/PhD/PostDoc)

If our research topics resonate with your interest, please do not hesitate to send us your application.

Please send a single PDF including: letter of motivation, CV, transcript of records, and namess of referees to application.priesemannATds.mpg.de.

Your motivation letter (1–2 pages) should summarize your past research achievements, skills, and interests, and include a short outline (~½ page) of “your research interest” — the topic you would like to pursue in our group (this not necessary for Bachelor and Master theses, but welcome). If you provide this, I will have a careful look at your application. The short outline does not need to be a final research proposal; it serves as basis for us to see the potential fit, and to discuss with you potential research projects in an interview.

Please add a short statement to which degree you have been using “AI” to prepare your letter of motivation.

We encourage applicants from all backgrounds and genders to contact us.
